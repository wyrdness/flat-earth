## Overview

The Flat Earth movement is a modern conspiracy theory community that rejects the spherical Earth model in favour of one of several flat-Earth cosmologies — most often a disc with the Arctic at the centre, an ice wall (interpreted as Antarctica) along the edge, and a dome enclosing a relatively small sun and moon. The movement traces its modern lineage to the Englishman Samuel Rowbotham (1816–1884), whose 1838 'Bedford Level' experiments and 1865 book Earth Not a Globe established 'Zetetic Astronomy'. Lady Elizabeth Blount's Universal Zetetic Society (founded 1893) carried the tradition into the 20th century; Samuel Shenton revived it in 1956 as the International Flat Earth Research Society, which Charles K. Johnson led from Lancaster, California, between 1971 and 2001. The contemporary movement re-emerged after 2009, when Daniel Shenton resurrected the Flat Earth Society as a web-based community, and grew rapidly between 2014 and 2019, propelled by YouTube algorithm dynamics and figures such as Mark Sargent, Mike Hughes, and rapper B.o.B. The 2018 documentary Behind the Curve (dir. Daniel J. Clark) followed several leading believers and notably recorded a 2017 ring-laser-gyroscope experiment in which their own apparatus measured the Earth's rotation. Although flat-Earth claims have been definitively refuted by everything from Eratosthenes' c. 240 BCE shadow measurement to satellite imagery, the modern movement persists as a sociological and online phenomenon and is the most-studied case in the contemporary literature on conspiracy belief.

## Etymology

- **Origin**: The English phrase 'flat earth' is descriptive. The term Zetetic, used by 19th-century proponents, derives from Greek zētēin, 'to seek', and was adopted by Samuel Rowbotham as the name of his investigative method.
- **Meaning**: 'Flat Earth' simply asserts that the Earth's surface is a plane rather than a curved spheroid. 'Zetetic' frames the project as one of empirical seeking opposed to deductive Newtonian science.
- **First use**: 1849 (Rowbotham's pamphlet Zetetic Astronomy, A Description of Several Experiments); 1865 (Rowbotham's Earth Not a Globe).

## Theories & Explanations

### Scientific

**Sociological-conspiracy explanation** _(Asheley R. Landrum, Anthony Olshansky, Alex Olshansky, John Paolillo)_

Researchers in science studies and communication treat flat-Earth belief as an emergent online conspiracy community sustained by recommendation algorithms, identity dynamics, and distrust of institutions, rather than by genuine empirical disagreement.

### Skeptical

**Direct empirical refutation** _(Mainstream physics, geodesy, and astronomy)_

Earth's spherical shape is supported by overlapping evidence from Eratosthenes (c. 240 BCE), the Bedford Level repetition by Wallace (1870), satellite imagery, GPS, gravitational physics, ship-disappearance over the horizon, lunar-eclipse geometry, and direct observation from orbit.

**Self-administered refutation in Behind the Curve** _(Documentary record (Clark 2018), Skeptical commentators (Phil Plait, Mick West))_

In the 2018 documentary Behind the Curve, the flat-Earth group's own ring-laser gyroscope detected a 15°/hour rotation — exactly Earth's spherical-rotation rate — which the experimenters then dismissed as instrument error.
